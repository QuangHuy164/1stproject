    Choux

    The butter is put inside the dough which is repeatedly folded and rolled out before baking. Choux pastry is just a
    mixed together dough that’s placed on a baking sheet in different form and baked.

    Some bakers prefer all purpose or bread flour because the high protein content makes the choux pastry hold
    their shape better. These flours also give choux pastry a thick and crisp shell.

    The word choux in French means cabbage, and choux pastry gets its name from the fact that the little balls
    of choux paste used to make cream puffs resemble little cabbages.

    Choux Pastry

    Choux pastry, also called pâte à choux ("pot-ah-SHOO"), is made with flour, butter, eggs, and water, although
    it can sometimes be made using half water and half milk. It is leavened with steam, rather than a chemical
    leavening agent such as baking soda or baking powder, or a biological leavening agent such as yeast.

    The dough is made on the stovetop, with the ingredients (except for the eggs) brought to a boil while stirring to form a ball. This is pretty unusual, since most pastry doughs are mixed and then rolled or folded, but not cooked.
